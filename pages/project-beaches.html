KARL BAYLY
if we opened me up we'd find beaches

RM Gallery, Auckland, NZ

Feburary, 2020
In Les plages d’Agnès 2008 (The Beaches of Agnès) an autobiographical essay by Agnes Varda, there is a scene where water crashes and rolls on the sand dunes. Varda speaks in French so the subtitles read “if we opened up people we’d find landscapes, if we opened me up we’d find beaches”. I think if we opened up people we find land- scapes and rock n roll, but that’s just me.

Sand is always moving, changed by wind or water or foot steps. Can shells carry with it the sound of where it is from? Like a way of find its way back or taking its place with it? The sound of the ocean against the sand, its falling over and washing away, the murmur of sand crisping under the sun? If you hold one over your ear you can hear the sound of the ocean. The ostrich foot shell (Takai / Struthiolaria papulosa) is where I first heard the ocean in a shell, miles from the sea. Different shells have different sounds, they are holding different landscapes within them.
